VibeLattice Documentation

VibeLattice is a browser-based aerodynamics simulation tool — a numerically faithful JavaScript/WASM port of AVL (Athena Vortex Lattice) by Mark Drela and Harold Youngren.

Getting Started

  1. Click Load AVL or choose a built-in example from the dropdown.
  2. Adjust flight conditions (velocity, CL, mass, etc.) in the Settings panel.
  3. Click Execute to run the vortex-lattice solver.
  4. View results in the Plots and Outputs panels.

Panels

Editor

View and edit the raw AVL geometry definition. Changes here are reflected live in the 3D preview. The editor supports AVL template variable syntax (${name:default}) for parameterized models.

Settings

Configure aircraft reference values (Sref, Cref, Bref), symmetry planes, flight conditions, control surface deflections, and constraint modes. You can also load .mass and .run files here.

Plots

3D geometry visualization and result plots including spanwise lift distributions, Trefftz-plane drag breakdown, stability derivatives, and eigenmode analysis.

Outputs

Tabular output of total forces/moments, stability derivatives, strip forces, and element-level data. Results can be downloaded as text files.

File Formats

ExtensionDescription
.avlAircraft geometry definition (surfaces, sections, airfoils)
.runRun-case file with flight conditions and constraints
.massMass distribution file for inertia and CG

Flight Conditions

  • Gravity — gravitational acceleration (default 9.81 m/s²)
  • Air density — freestream air density (default 1.225 kg/m³)
  • Mass — total aircraft mass (kg)
  • Velocity — freestream velocity (m/s)
  • CL — target lift coefficient
  • Flight mode — level/banked flight or looping

Constraint Modes

The solver trims the aircraft by satisfying constraints. You can constrain angle of attack (alpha), sideslip (beta), roll rate, and control surface deflections. Each variable can be set, or used to achieve a target value.

Symmetry

  • IYSym — Y-symmetry: mirror geometry in the X-Z plane
  • IZSym — Z-symmetry: mirror geometry in the X-Y plane

Using symmetry reduces computation by modeling only half (or quarter) of the aircraft.

Strip Forces Output

The strip forces file contains aerodynamic results for each spanwise strip. All force and moment coefficients are referred to the strip's own area and chord, making them dimensionless and comparable across strips of different sizes.

ColumnDefinition
strip_idx Global strip index (1 to Nstrip), numbered sequentially across all surfaces.
surface_idx Index of the surface that contains this strip.
Xle X coordinate of the strip leading edge.
Yle Y coordinate of the strip leading edge (spanwise position).
Zle Z coordinate of the strip leading edge (vertical position).
Chord Local chord length of the strip.
Area Strip planform area (Chord × strip width).
c_cl Span loading: normal force coefficient in the strip's Trefftz (far-wake) plane. Used in induced drag calculation.
ai Induced angle — downwash angle at the strip in the Trefftz plane, computed from the wake influence of all other strips.
cl_norm Local lift coefficient normalized by the velocity component perpendicular to the strip span direction (cl / Vperp²).
cl Local section lift coefficient in strip coordinates (force perpendicular to freestream and span, referred to strip area).
cd Local section drag coefficient in strip coordinates (force along freestream direction, referred to strip area). Includes induced drag.
cdv Viscous (profile) drag coefficient from airfoil polar data, if specified.
cm_c/4 Pitching moment coefficient about the strip quarter-chord point, referred to strip area and chord.
cm_LE Pitching moment coefficient about the strip leading edge, referred to strip area and chord.
C.P.x/c Center of pressure location as a fraction of chord from the leading edge. Computed as 0.25 − cmc/4 / cl.
